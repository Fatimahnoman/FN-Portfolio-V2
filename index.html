AI Prompting 2026

13 Concepts I've Mastered

From the Panaversity AI Agent Factory curriculum — the complete foundation of professional AI use in 2026.

The official AI Prompting 2026 crash course covers 13 concepts in 4 parts. I've studied, practiced, and can teach every one of them.

✦ Claude
💎 Gemini
📘 NotebookLM
1
How AI Knows Things
1
Novice vs Power User
The difference between a one-line question and a full brief with context, files, and constraints.
2
Pretrained Knowledge
What AI learned from internet text — and where its blind spots are (niche, private, recent data).
3
3 Retrieval Modes
When AI answers from memory, fires off a web search, or runs deep research across dozens of sources.
2
Talking to AI Well
4
Context is the Whole Game
The 5-layer context stack — system prompt, tools, your prompt, history, and uploaded files.
5
Reasoning (Think Hard)
Using extended thinking modes (o1, Claude's thinking) for complex multi-trade-off problems.
6
Sycophancy Awareness
Recognising when AI agrees to please rather than to inform — and fixing it with neutral framing.
7
Writing Great Prompts
Structure, constraints, audience, output format — the discipline of briefing AI like a colleague.
3
Real Workflows
8
Deep Research
Directing AI to do structured, multi-source research with source quality control and citations.
9
AI Image Generation
Crafting prompts that produce consistent, intentional visual results.
10
Data Analysis with AI
Uploading files and asking AI to extract insights, patterns, and summaries.
4
Beyond Chat
11
Desktop Apps & AI Assistants
Using Claude Desktop, ChatGPT app, Gemini apps, Cowork, and OpenWork for real productivity.
12
Tool Use & Function Calling
Connecting AI to external tools, APIs, and services to automate real tasks.
13
Choosing the Right Model
Knowing when to use ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — and understanding system prompts, projects, and context budgets.
